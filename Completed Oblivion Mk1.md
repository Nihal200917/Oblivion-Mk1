# Oblivion Mk1 Completed

It competed in its first competition at TRC Test n Tune and lasted 3 rounds. More than what was expected compared to several factors of other bots.
## Things that went good
The bot worked fine. It turned on, could move, but had a failed failsafe in the beggining ,which was fixed later. It lasted 3 rounds without major damage to chassis until 3 round.
## What did not work out so well
- The bot was too slow, other bots ran on 3s and 4s lipo with a motor that produced ~1200 rpm while Oblivion ran on 150 rpm with 2s lipo 1000mah (i didn't need to charge it after every round)
- The bot shouldn't have been made entirely with TPU. It should have had a pla sturdy Chassis to support hardware and give it structure
- Just being a wedge = impossible to get points. Oblivion needs to make some damage on other bots or be strong enough to push them around and pin them
- MAJOR: WHEELS ALWAYS KEPT FLYING OUT. Wheels weren't mounted securely so everytime they would fall off and most of the time they lost traction so Oblivion was completely helpless in the arena.
- mount motor securely and tightly
- USE A SINGLE BIDIRECTIONAL DUAL WAY ESC AND A SMALLER BATTERY (3-4s Lipo 300 mah rechargable)
- Direct drive is easy but when wheels are exposed the motors take hit especially if they are weak like N20 motors
- NEED TO BUY NEW MOTORS (MORE POWERFUL)
- Reciever should have been smaller
